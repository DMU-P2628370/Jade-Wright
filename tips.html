Safety Tips In The Kitchen

Here are a few simple tips that will increase your safety while in the kitchen. This is especially good for beginners, but also a good reminder for experienced chefs or people who spend a lot of time in the kitchen as these saefty precautions can easily slip away with time and comfort.

Knives and Chopping

  • Always concentrate. Never engage in coversation while chopping.

  • Use a chopping board. Peferably colour coded for specific foods (go to sanitising within the Hygeine section)

  • Always use sharp knives. Duller/blunt knives are harder to work with causing more accidents

Fire Hazzards

  • Never leave tea towels close to the hob

  • Don't leave food cooking unattended

  • Don't leave wooden spoons hanging over the edge of saucepans

  • Always make sure that your fire alarms are working

Hygeine In The Kitchen

Sanitising

  • Use specific chopping boards for meat and vegetables to avoid cross contamination

  • Sanitise all work top surafces before and after use

  • Always wash hands before and after touching any food

  • Wash all fruit and vegetables to get rid of bacteria and germs already on there

Personal Hygeine

  • Wash hands

  • Wear an apron to avoid germs from clothes getting into food

  • Tie all hair up or wear a hair net

  • Wear a blue plaster if you have any cuts

  • Roll sleeves up to aviod germs from clothing touching the food
